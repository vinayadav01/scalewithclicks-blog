# Search Campaign vs Display Campaign: Which Should You Choose?

If you're planning to advertise your business using Google Ads, one of the first decisions you'll make is choosing between a **Search Campaign** and a **Display Campaign**.

Both campaign types can generate leads and sales, but they work in completely different ways. Selecting the wrong campaign often leads to wasted budget, poor-quality traffic, and disappointing results.

We've seen businesses spend thousands of dollars on Display campaigns when they actually needed Search campaigns—or rely only on Search when Display remarketing could have significantly increased conversions.

In this guide, we'll compare Search Campaigns and Display Campaigns in detail, explain when each works best, and help you decide which option aligns with your marketing goals.

If you're new to Google Ads, consider reading our complete **[Google Ads Setup Guide for Beginners](/blog/google-ads-setup-guide)** before launching your first campaign.

## What Is a Google Search Campaign?

![Google Search Ads Example](/images/google-search-ad-example.jpg)

A Search Campaign displays text advertisements on Google's search results page when someone searches for keywords related to your business.

For example:

- "Google Ads agency"
- "best digital marketing company"
- "running shoes online"
- "plumber near me"

When users search these terms, Google can show your ad above the organic search results.

Unlike many other advertising platforms, Search Campaigns target users who are actively looking for a solution, making them one of the highest-intent advertising formats available.

---

Search campaigns offer several advantages:

- Capture high-intent users
- Generate qualified leads
- Increase website inquiries
- Drive phone calls
- Improve online sales
- Easier conversion tracking
- Better ROI for service-based businesses

If your goal is immediate lead generation, Search campaigns are usually the best starting point.

## What Is a Display Campaign?

![Google Display Network Example](/images/google-display-network-example.jpg)

Display Campaigns show visual advertisements across millions of websites, blogs, mobile apps, Gmail, and YouTube that are part of the Google Display Network (GDN).

Instead of appearing when users perform a search, Display Ads appear while people browse content online.

These ads may include:

- Banner ads
- Responsive display ads
- Animated creatives
- Image ads
- Rich media advertisements

Display campaigns help businesses reach potential customers before they actively begin searching.

---

## Benefits of Display Campaigns

Display campaigns are ideal for:

- Brand awareness
- Product launches
- Building trust
- Reaching large audiences
- Remarketing website visitors
- Increasing repeat purchases
- Supporting the customer journey

Businesses with visually appealing products often benefit significantly from Display advertising.

## Understanding User Intent

The biggest difference between the two campaign types is **user intent**.

### Search Campaign

Someone searches:

> "Emergency dentist near me"

This user already needs a dentist.

They're actively looking to book an appointment.

Your ad appears exactly when they're ready to take action.

---

### Display Campaign

Someone is reading a health blog and sees an advertisement for a dental clinic.

They may not need treatment today.

Your ad simply introduces your business.

The conversion may happen weeks later.

This is why Search campaigns generally produce higher conversion rates.

## Search Campaign Cost vs Display Campaign Cost

Generally speaking:

### Search Campaign

- Higher CPC
- Better conversion rates
- Higher purchase intent

### Display Campaign

- Lower CPC
- Lower conversion rate
- Larger audience reach

Although Search campaigns often cost more per click, they usually generate more valuable traffic.

A cheaper click doesn't always mean better ROI.

## Which Campaign Converts Better?

For most businesses:

Search Campaigns typically outperform Display Campaigns when the objective is direct conversions.

That's because users searching on Google already have intent.

Display campaigns, however, excel at nurturing users throughout longer buying journeys.

Businesses with long sales cycles often use both campaign types together.

## Common Mistakes Businesses Make

### 1. Starting with Display Instead of Search

New advertisers often assume more impressions mean better performance.

In reality, Search campaigns usually deliver higher-quality traffic.

---

### 2. Ignoring Remarketing

Many businesses spend money attracting visitors but never re-engage them.

Remarketing often produces some of the highest ROAS across Google Ads.

---

### 3. Poor Creative Design

Display campaigns rely heavily on strong visuals.

Low-quality banner designs usually receive fewer clicks.

---

### 4. Wrong Campaign Objective

Running Display campaigns for immediate lead generation often leads to disappointing results.

Campaign objectives should always align with business goals.

---

### 5. No Conversion Tracking

Without proper tracking, it's impossible to know which campaign is generating real business results.

If you haven't already, make sure your account includes GA4, Google Tag Manager, and conversion tracking before scaling campaigns.

# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## Is Search Campaign better than Display Campaign?

For direct lead generation and sales, Search Campaigns generally perform better because they target users actively searching for products or services.

---

## Are Display campaigns worth running?

Yes. Display campaigns are highly effective for brand awareness, remarketing, and increasing customer recall.

---

## Which campaign has a lower CPC?

Display campaigns typically have a lower average cost per click than Search campaigns.

---

## Can I run Search and Display campaigns together?

Absolutely. Many successful advertisers use Search campaigns to capture demand and Display campaigns to nurture and re-engage visitors.

---

## Which campaign is better for local businesses?

Most local businesses benefit more from Search campaigns because they target customers actively looking for nearby services.

---

## Is remarketing available in Search campaigns?

Remarketing is primarily associated with Display campaigns, although Google also offers Remarketing Lists for Search Ads (RLSA), allowing advertisers to customize bids and ads for previous website visitors.

---

## Final Thoughts

There isn't a single "best" campaign type for every business. The right choice depends on your goals, budget, audience, and stage of growth.

If your objective is generating immediate leads or sales, Search Campaigns are usually the strongest option. If you're looking to build awareness, stay visible, or bring previous visitors back to your website, Display Campaigns play an important supporting role.

Many of the highest-performing Google Ads accounts don't choose one over the other—they combine both strategically. By using Search to capture demand and Display to reinforce your brand through remarketing, you can create a more complete customer journey and maximize your advertising investment.

Whether you're just getting started or looking to improve an existing account, building campaigns around clear objectives and accurate conversion tracking will always produce better long-term results.
