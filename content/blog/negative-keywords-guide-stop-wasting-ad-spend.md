# Negative Keywords Guide: Stop Wasting Ad Spend & Improve Google Ads ROI

Every advertiser wants more conversions while spending less on ads. Yet one of the biggest reasons businesses lose money in Google Ads isn't poor ad copy or low budgets—it's showing ads for the wrong searches.

If you've ever wondered why your campaigns receive plenty of clicks but very few leads or sales, there's a good chance you're missing a solid **negative keyword strategy**.

Negative keywords help Google understand **which searches should NOT trigger your ads**. Instead of paying for irrelevant traffic, you can focus your budget on people who are actually looking for your products or services.

Whether you're running campaigns yourself or working with a professional **[Google Ads Agency](https://scalewithclicks.com/services/google-ads-agency)**, mastering negative keywords can significantly improve your campaign performance.

---

## What Are Negative Keywords?

Negative keywords are search terms that prevent your ads from appearing when someone searches for those words or phrases.

![What are Negative Keywords in Google Ads](/images/what-are-negative-keywords.jpg)


For example, if you sell premium CRM software, you probably don't want your ads appearing for searches like:

- free CRM software
- CRM jobs
- CRM internship
- CRM course
- CRM PDF

Adding words like **free**, **jobs**, **internship**, **course**, and **PDF** as negative keywords ensures your ads only appear for potential customers instead of people looking for something entirely different.

---

## Why Negative Keywords Matter

Many advertisers spend hours optimizing bids, testing headlines, and improving landing pages while completely ignoring search intent.

Negative keywords solve several expensive problems at once.

![Reduce Wasted Google Ads Budget](/images/reduce-wasted-ad-spend.jpg)


### 1. Reduce Wasted Ad Spend

Every irrelevant click costs money.

Blocking poor-quality searches means more of your budget goes toward users who are likely to convert.

---

### 2. Increase Click-Through Rate (CTR)

When your ads appear only for relevant searches, users are more likely to click.

Higher CTR often improves your Quality Score.

---

### 3. Improve Conversion Rate

Better targeting means visitors arrive with stronger purchase intent.

As irrelevant traffic decreases, conversion rates generally improve.

---

### 4. Improve Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)

Instead of paying for curiosity clicks, your budget focuses on qualified prospects.

That often leads to lower CPA and higher ROAS.

## How Google Uses Negative Keywords

Whenever someone performs a search, Google compares that search with:

- Your keywords
- Your negative keywords

If the search contains one of your negative keywords (depending on match type), Google prevents your ad from showing.

Think of it as adding filters that remove unwanted traffic before you pay for it.

---

![Negative Keyword Match Types](/images/negative-keyword-match-types.jpg)


## Types of Negative Keyword Match Types

Understanding match types is essential.

### Negative Broad Match

This is the default option.

Example:

Negative keyword:

```
free shoes
```

Your ad won't show for:

- free shoes
- shoes free

But it could still show for:

- free running sneakers

---

### Negative Phrase Match

Example:

```
"free shoes"
```

Your ad won't show if the search contains that exact phrase.

Examples blocked:

- free shoes online
- best free shoes

---

### Negative Exact Match

Example:

```
[free shoes]
```

Only blocks the exact search.

Searches like:

- free shoes online

may still trigger your ads.

## Where to Find Negative Keywords

One of the best sources is the **Search Terms Report**.

Go to:

Google Ads → Campaign → Insights & Reports → Search Terms

This report shows exactly what people searched before clicking your ad.

Look for searches that:

- Never convert
- Have high spend
- Are unrelated to your business
- Indicate research rather than buying intent

These become excellent candidates for your negative keyword list.

![Google Ads Search Terms Report](/images/google-ads-search-terms-report.jpg)

## Don't Add Too Many Negative Keywords

Some advertisers go overboard.

For example:

Adding:

```
cheap
```

might accidentally block users searching for:

> Affordable CRM software for small business

which could still convert.

Always check whether a keyword is genuinely hurting campaign performance before blocking it.

---

## Monitor Search Terms Regularly

Negative keyword optimization isn't a one-time task.

Successful advertisers review search terms:

- Weekly for new campaigns
- Every two weeks for active accounts
- Monthly for mature campaigns

Small improvements compound over time.

---

## Mistakes to Avoid

### Ignoring Search Terms

Many advertisers never review search queries after launching campaigns.

This often becomes the biggest source of wasted spend.

![Google Ads Optimization Mistakes](/images/google-ads-mistakes.jpg)


---

### Using Only Broad Match Keywords

Broad match without proper negatives can generate irrelevant traffic quickly.

Balance broad targeting with strong exclusions.

---

### Blocking Valuable Keywords

Always verify before adding a negative keyword.

One incorrect exclusion can eliminate profitable traffic.

---

### Forgetting Brand Variations

If you're running campaigns for different products, avoid accidentally blocking branded searches that convert well.

---

## Advanced Tips for Better Campaign Performance

### Segment Campaigns by Intent

Separate campaigns for:

- Commercial keywords
- Informational keywords
- Brand keywords

Each campaign can have its own negative keyword strategy.

---

### Use Competitor Names Carefully

If competitor searches never convert, consider adding them as negatives.

However, test first before excluding them permanently.

---

### Review Performance After Adding Negatives

Watch metrics like:

- CTR
- CPC
- Conversion Rate
- CPA
- ROAS

The goal isn't simply fewer clicks.

It's more qualified clicks.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## What are negative keywords in Google Ads?

Negative keywords prevent your ads from appearing for irrelevant searches. They help reduce wasted ad spend and improve campaign performance by filtering out low-intent traffic.

---

## How often should I update my negative keywords?

For most accounts, reviewing the Search Terms Report every week or every two weeks is recommended. High-budget campaigns may require even more frequent monitoring.

---

## Where can I find negative keyword ideas?

The Search Terms Report in Google Ads is the best place to identify irrelevant queries. You can also use Google Search suggestions, Keyword Planner, competitor research, and your own customer insights.

---

## Can negative keywords improve Quality Score?

Yes. By preventing irrelevant impressions and clicks, negative keywords often increase CTR and improve Quality Score over time.

---

## Should every campaign have its own negative keywords?

Yes. While shared negative keyword lists are useful, each campaign should also have campaign-specific negatives based on its targeting and objectives.

---

## Can too many negative keywords hurt performance?

Absolutely. Blocking valuable search terms by mistake can reduce impressions and conversions. Always review search intent before adding new exclusions.
